Man to the Moon
Manifesto Poster
Next Project
“…I believe that this nation should commit itself to achieving the goal, before this decade is out, of landing a man on the moon and returning him safely to the earth. No single space project in this period will be more impressive to mankind, or more important for the long-range exploration of space; and none will be so difficult or expensive to accomplish.”

The manifesto “Man to the Moon” is from a speech called "We choose to go to the Moon,” given by United States President John F. Kennedy on September 12the, 1961. The purpose of the speech is to secure funding for significant US projects to boost the US economy, support democracy over communism, diminish the threat of nuclear weapons, and land a man on the moon.
Although this is a manifesto from 1961, I wish to design it from the perspective of someone currently in 2021. The poster is intended to be more futuristic because I hope to convey that we are presently living in the past’s future. Sending a man to the moon was such an impactful event in 1961 but is so common nowadays.
